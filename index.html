The curious economist

A few years ago, a stranger asked me something simple over dinner. Why doesn't the government just print money and give it to everyone? No malice in the question, no politics either. Just an honest thought from someone who lives in the real world. And honestly, it's not a silly question. Governments can print money. So why not?

Here's the thing. I have a PhD in Economics. I've spent over ten years teaching it in classrooms. So yes, I had an answer. What I didn't have was a way to say it in one breath, without graphs, without jargon, without turning dinner into a lecture.

That moment stuck with me. I love research, I love the classroom, but I've noticed something. We academics are trained to write for each other, and somewhere along the way, we stop asking whether the person outside the field can actually read what we wrote. I don't think that's anyone's fault. I just think it's worth fixing.

So that's what this series is. Short stories, not textbooks. Six or seven chapters, five minutes each, thirty pages, start to finish. No equations. Just the same answers, minus everything that made them feel out of reach.
